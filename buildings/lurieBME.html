Ann and Robert H. Lurie Biomedical Engineering Building

History

As part of the expansion of UM's Biomedical Engineering Department, the Ann and Robert H. Lurie Biomedical Engineering Building officially opened in August of 2006. It was part of an expansion of the University of Michigan Biomedical Engineering Department (BME). The building is home to facilities that specialize in cellular and molecular biotechnologies interactive classrooms, research labs, and support space. The laboratory architecture of LBME is characterized by "open space" designed to facilitate collabaoration between researchers. The building is named after Chicagoans Robert H. Lurie, a real estate and investment magnate who died from colon cancer in 1990, at age 48,and his wife, Ann.

Fun Facts

The "Tilted Donut with S (2006)" is a sculpture on the University of Michigan North Campus, located on the south lawn of Lurie Biomedical Engineering Building. It is the work of sculptor Fletcher Benton, and was donated by the Class of 1956 Engineering.

Artwork helps engineers to be inspired and keep tehir creative juices flowing. The presence of various designs and color schemes in the hallway of Lurie is a positive influence on the minds its researchers.

The atrium of Lurie is warm, welcoming, and inviting. With several earthtones and hanging scultpures, this is a great, place to study, eat, and collaborate.
