Climate and Space Research Building

History

The department had not be named Climate and Space Sciences and Engineering (Climate & Space or simply CLaSP) until the late 1960s, it was named Atmospheric, Oceanic and Space Sciences (AOSS) before this change. The rename was caused by adding the disciplines of meteorology and space sciences to the department of AOSS. AOSS was derived from the combination of the interdepartmental graduate program in Aeronomy and the department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Science (AOS) in 1966. The blending history of individual departments into a large department was to put the department and its students and faculties at the forefront of research with a whole systemic view which includes the understanding of the Earth, atmosphere, planets, solar system and space weather.

Fun Facts

There is a sculpture on the wall in CSRB. It visualizes the coronal mass ejections (CMEs) through translations of data and scientific models.

A beautiful yard is surrounded by the CSR building. Tables and chairs are provided for students and faculties to take a rest at this scenery place.

There is a hallway in CSRB where walls on both sides shows posters introducing female scientists and their achievements.

Some beautiful pictures of comet in night sky are hanging on the wall.

Some pictures of inventions and history that are related to CLaSP are exhibited on the walls.

Near the front door of the building, there is a place with comfortable chairs for people to sit and have discussion, work, or have rest.
