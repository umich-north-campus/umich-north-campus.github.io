Mortimer E. Cooley Building

History

Home to the Nuclear Engineering Department, here at the University of Michigan, Cooley Building host variety of unique labs: Plasma Lab, Ion Beam Lab, Neutron Lab and many others. It is also the only place on campus that has a live Geiger counter, informing all students and faculty about the radiation levels in Ann Arbor

Fun Facts

Interestingly enough, the Mortimer E. Cooley Building is the first structure to be built on the region we now know as North Campus. Named in honor of Michigan Engineering’s well-loved former dean Mortimer Elwyn Cooley (1904 through 1928), the structure was completed in time for Michigan Engineering’s centennial celebration. Cooley palyed a prominent role in who played a major role in establishing the (ERI) Engineering Research Institute. In the years to come, this lone research institute would be joined by several others.

On the first floor stands a bust of building honoree, Mortimer Elwyn Cooley.
