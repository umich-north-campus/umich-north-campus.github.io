North Campus Research Complex

History

In 1957, some of the property located at the north campus that belonged to University of Michigan was sold to Parke-Davis pharmaceutical company, which was acquired by Pfizer in 2000. In 2002, U of M sold additional area to Pfizer. However, those property were sold back to U of M in 2009, and the site was named North Campus Research Complex. The motivation for U of M to purchase the former pharmaceutical research campus was that they wanted to bring scientists and entrepreneurs together, so that research that is done today will be turned into medications, devices and technologies tomorrow.

Fun Facts

You can even find a gym inside the NCRC buildings. The gym includes locker room, dancing room, and a weight-lifting training room. Shower rooms are also located beside the locker room.

Many areas for students and faculties to relax, eat, and have discussions can be found in the buildings in the NCRC. Some of them are located at scenery places.

There is a long history wall in the basement of NCRC. The wall introduces the history of health services research at U of M.

There are sculptures on the aisle inside a building of NCRC and at the outdoor of NCRC. There is a display board explaining the design of the sculptures outdoor. It explains that the sculptures were designed to express the search, the research, the persistence, the power and the evolution of scientific process.

There is a small market and a coffee shop in NCRC for students and faculties to have a cup of coffee or to have snacks or meals when they are hungry.

An interactive installation that blends art and technology is displayed in NCRC. The exhibit is integrated with sensors that help the installation respond to activities around it, including movement and sound. The installation will present different light patterns as response.
