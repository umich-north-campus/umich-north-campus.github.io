Francois-Xavier Bagnoud Building

History

François-Xavier Bagnoud (FXB) Building is where the Aerospace Engineering Department locates. The building was donated by the FXB foundation, which was founded to honor the death of François-Xavier Bagnoud, an alumnus of U of M. François completed his bachelor’s degree within three years and wrote “Helicopter Theory for Private Pilots” during his undergraduate. François was passionate about rescuing people but unfortunately was killed in a helicopter accident on January 14, 1986, less than four years after his graduation from U of M.

Fun Facts

There are a lot of aerospace related exhibits at the first floor of FXB This wall exhibits items related to an UM alumnus, Clarence L. “Kelly” Johnson, an American aeronautical and systems engineer. He is recognized for his contributions to a series of important aircraft designs, most notably the Lockheed U-2 and SR-71 Blackbird. The wall shows the awards he received, the books he wrote, and his diplomas from UMich.

This wall displays the outer space programs that U of M alumni have joined and the awards they have received. In the middle of the wall is a portion of a lunar sample returned by Apollo 17 astronauts.

The model of a U of M’s solar car is displayed. U of M Solar Car Team has been designing and racing solar vehicles since 1989. Plenty of awards have been received by the Solar Car Team.

The composite fan blade on display is one of the blades that help propel the world’s largest jet engine. Its efficient design helps the engine to be one of the most fuel efficient and quietest engines.

An aircraft that was built by U of M professor Edgar J. Lesher is displayed. His airplane design course was widely known, and two airplanes in the aero departmental laboratories was designed and built by him.

Several engine airflow FADEC controls and combustion-related modules are displayed.
