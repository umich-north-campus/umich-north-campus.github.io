James and Anne Duderstadt Center

History

The James and Anne Duderstadt Center, which was formerly known as the Media Union, was designed by Albert Kahn Associates of Detroit. It opened in 1996 as a place to provide collaborative environment for faculty and students at the University of Michigan. It is the largest public computing site at the university. In addition to more than 400 computers available 24/7, it also provides various sources to students in different disciplines.

Fun Facts

The Mujo Cafe provides a space for refreshment and social interaction. Chairs and tables are available next to Mujo.

The room is located in the lower level of Duderstadt Center. A wide variety of games from the 1970s to the present could be found in this place. It is open from Monday to Friday, which allows you to relieve stress in your busy school life.
